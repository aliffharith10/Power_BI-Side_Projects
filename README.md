**Exercise 1: AdventureWorks**
Objectives:
1. To Monitor Global Sales Performance and Revenue Trends
2. To Optimize Product Mix and Minimize Returns
3. To Analyze Customer Demographics and Value

Findings:
1. Total revenue reached $24.9M with a profit of $10.5M. The "Revenue Trending" chart shows a significant upward trajectory starting in mid-2021 and peaking in early 2022.
2. The map visualization indicates that the United States and Australia are the largest markets by sales volume (indicated by the bubble size), followed by smaller clusters in Europe (UK, France, Germany).
3. Accessories are the highest volume category with 17.0K orders, followed by Bikes (13.9K). However, "Tires and Tubes" is the single most ordered product type.
4. The overall return rate is 2.2%, but specific items like the "Sport-100 Helmet" (Red and Blue) have high individual return rates of 3.33% and 3.31%. "Shorts" are identified as the "Most Returned Product Type," suggesting a potential sizing or quality issue in that line.
5. Revenue is heavily driven by customers in Professional (7.9K orders) and Skilled Manual (6.0K orders) occupations. Interestingly, the "Average" income level group contributes more orders (11.6K) than the "High" income group (2.8K).
6. There are 17.4K unique customers with an average revenue of $1,431 per customer. Top individual contributors, like Mr. Maurice Shan (who spent $12.4K), represent a critical "Power User" segment for the business.

**Exercise 2: Financial Sample**
Objectives:
1. To Analyze Global Profitability and Regional Contributions
2. To Evaluate Sales Performance Across Segments
3. To Identify Product-Specific Sales Drivers

Findings:
1. France and Canada are the top profit contributors, followed closely by Germany. The United States and Mexico show lower overall profit margins in comparison.
2. The "Profit by Month and Year" line chart shows a highly cyclical pattern. There were significant peaks in October 2013 and October 2024, with a notable dip in profitability around May 2014.
3. Across almost every product category (Paseo, VTT, Velo, etc.), the Government segment is the largest driver of sales volume.
4. The Small Business segment is the second most significant contributor, showing particularly strong performance in the "Paseo" and "VTT" product lines.
5. Paseo is clearly the top-performing product, generating the highest sales volume (peaking near $15M in the Government segment alone).

**Exercise 3: Patient**
Objectives:
1. To Monitor Wait List Volume and Year-over-Year Growth
2. To Categorize Patient Backlog by Case Severity and Age
3. To Identify Critical Specialties and Long-Term Waiters

Findings:
1. The "Latest Month Wait List" stands at 709K, which is a significant increase from the 640K recorded in the previous year (PY).
2. The "Monthly Trend Analysis" shows that while "Day Case" and "Inpatient" numbers have remained relatively stable, the Outpatient waitlist has experienced a sharp, consistent climb from mid-2018 through early 2021, peaking at over 0.6M cases.
3. The vast majority of the waitlist, about 72.49% (80.21K average), is composed of Outpatient cases.
4. The 16-64 age profile represents the largest group of waiting patients (as seen in the detail table with over 309K entries for Jan 2018).
5. A concerning finding in the "Time Band vs Age Profile" chart is that the 18+ Months category is the most populated band, indicating a high volume of long-term waiting patients across all age groups.
6. Ophthalmology, Paediatric Orthopaedic, and Paediatric Respiratory Medicine are among the top specialties with high waitlist numbers, each averaging around 35-36 cases in the top 5 ranking.
7. While many patients fall into the "0-3 Months" band, the "18+ Months" bar is significantly taller than the intermediate bands (3-6, 6-9, 9-12 months), suggesting that once a patient passes the one-year mark, they are likely to remain on the list for a very long time.
